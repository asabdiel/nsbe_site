Event Ideas

Below is a list of potential events. Note that costs and attendee numbers can vary based on your chapter's resources.

Event Description Potential Cost Ideal Attendees Tips
General Body Meeting (GBM) An event where the chapter's executive board meets with membership to exchange information about chapter goals, future events, etc. $0 50 People tend to not want to be lectured so include a fun activity or pose questions to keep them engaged. Ideally if the first GBM is an activity it will help leave a positive impression.
Academic Tech Bowl An event where students answer jeopardy style questions ranging from various STEM related courses. $100 - $400 (Trophies, Food, Prizes) 50 Use buzzinga.io or free alternatives. Engage with PCI members or local high schools.
Let's Debate An event where teams debate a particular subject, ideally related to professionalism and current professional world events. $100-$400 (Trophies, Food, Prizes) 50 Engage with PCI members or local high schools.
Technical Workshops Workshops that teach a hard skill to boost resumes or learn new topics. $0-$500 (depending on skill) 20-30 Rely on industry professionals or companies with certifications.
Professionalism Workshop A workshop on soft skills to prepare for conferences and professional opportunities. $0-$300 (Catering) 50+ Utilize NSBE professionals to establish relationships.
Career Panel Industry professionals answer questions from membership. $0-$200 (Catering) 30-40 Utilize professional chapters for knowledge sharing.
Corporate Informational A company shares information about opportunities. $100-$300 (Catering) 40-50 Rely on corporate sponsors and local companies.
Career Fair Companies offer networking and job opportunities. $300-$1200 (Booths, Food) 50-100 Utilize sponsors and geographical connections.
Gala A formal event celebrating chapter successes. $500-$1500 (Supplies, Food) 50+ Invite from other colleges for networking. Include in sponsorship perks.
Hack-a-thon Students design technical solutions to real problems in teams. $200-$400 (Food, Prizes) 30+ Use corporate sponsors and collaborate with other STEM organizations.
